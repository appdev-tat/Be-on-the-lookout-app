When you report trafficking, those you contact will work with the FBI and local police to rescue the victims. Approaching traffickers is dangerous for you and their victims, and could lead to problems in the prosecution of the traffickers.

Call the hotline (USA)
(1-888-373-7888)
Call the hotline (Canada)
(1-888-222-TIPS)
Send a report via email to
report@polarisproject.org

If you are at a truck stop/travel plaza, please notify the manager-on-duty of the suspicious activity—he/she can assist in ending the trafficking activity.


Actionable Information

Detailed information will greatly help the prosecution of traffickers. Try to report as much of the following info about the incident as you can.

Take photos if possible.

Cars

Are there cars involved in the trafficking incident?

Record the make, model, color, license plate, etc.

People

Who is involved in the incident?

Make note of height, weight, ethnicity, hair color, eye color, age, etc.

Date/time

When did the incident happen?

What day and what time?

Location

Where did the incident take place?

Be as specific as you can.
