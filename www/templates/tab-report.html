Report suspicious activity

Immediately: If you’re watching a crime in progress, call 911 and then call the hotline. If you’re at a place of business, please notify the manager-on-duty of the suspicious activity; she/he needs to be aware of what is taking place on the property and assist in ending it.

Warning: Please do not approach traffickers. Allow law enforcement to deal with traffickers and recover victims. Approaching traffickers is not only dangerous for you and their victims but could lead to problems in the eventual prosecution of traffickers.

National Human Trafficking Hotline (USA) Advocates are available 24/7 to take reports of potential human trafficking. All reports are confidential and you may remain anonymous. Interpreters are available. The information you provide will be reviewed by the National Hotline and forwarded to specialized law enforcement and/or service providers where appropriate.

What to report

For law enforcement to open an investigation on your tip, they need "actionable information." This would include:

Vehicles

Are there any vehicles involved in the suspicious activity?

Record the make, model, color, license plate number, etc.

People

Who is involved in the activity?

Make note of height, weight, ethnicity, hair color, eye color, age, etc.

Take a picture, if you can, to show to law enforcement when they arrive on scene.

Date and time

When did the suspicious activity happen?

What day and what time?

Location

Where did the suspicious activity take place?

Do you have an address? Be as specific as you can.
