About human trafficking

Human trafficking is the exploitation of human beings for the purposes of forced labor or commercial sex. Traffickers use force, fraud and coercion to control their victims and make a profit off of their labor. Human trafficking is, indeed, modern-day slavery.

Trafficking can occur in many locations, including bus terminals, restaurants, hotels/motels, strip clubs, casinos, private homes, truck stops, rest stops, etc. Moreover, traffickers recruit victims out of bus terminals and utilize the bus itself to transport their victims. Whether you work behind a ticket counter, conduct bus maintenance, or are a professional driver, members of the bus industry are in a position to spot potential human trafficking situations and save a life.

If you see a minor working any of the areas mentioned above or suspect that the person selling sex is under someone else's control in order to make a quota, please report your tip now.
