Red-flag indicators

Somebody may be a current victim of human trafficking if he/she:

  • has restricted or controlled communication— not allowed to speak for self
  • has a disheveled or unkempt appearance, is alone, scared, or crying
  • is a minor traveling without adult supervision
  • offers to exchange sex for a ride, meal, etc.
  • does not know the person who is picking him/her up
  • shows any acknowledgement that she/he has a pimp and is making a quota
  • has signs of branding or tattooing of the trafficker's name (often on the neck)
  • has a lack of knowledge of the community or his/her whereabouts
  • is not in control of his/her own identification documents (ID/passport)

If you suspect someone is a victim of human trafficking, here are some questions to ask him/her:

  • Do you know the person who is picking you up?
  • Do you feel safe with the person you're traveling with?
  • Do your parents/siblings/relatives know where you are? If not, why not?
  • Are you free to come and go as you please?
  • Are you or your family being threatened? What is the nature of the threats?
